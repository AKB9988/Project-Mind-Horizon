JEE Mains & Advanced

JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) is a national-level entrance exam in India for admission to undergraduate engineering programs at prestigious institutions like IITs, NITs, and other top engineering colleges. It is divided into two stages: JEE Mains and JEE Advanced.

JEE Mains is the first stage, testing candidates on subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. It serves as a gateway to NITs, IIITs, and other centrally funded technical institutions. JEE Mains also acts as a qualifying exam for JEE Advanced.

JEE Advanced is the second stage, exclusively for candidates who clear JEE Mains. It is more challenging and is the entrance exam for the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), offering a higher level of difficulty to assess in-depth understanding and problem-solving skills in engineering subjects.

JEE Exam 2025 Overview

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is a highly competitive entrance test in India, aimed at evaluating the knowledge and aptitude of students aspiring to join undergraduate engineering programs. JEE acts as a gateway to prestigious engineering institutes like the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs). Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), this exam ensures transparency and maintains high standards for engineering education in India.

Types of JEE Main Exam

The JEE is divided into two main types:

  • The exam will consist of three papers: Paper 1 for BTech/BE, Paper 2A for BArch, and Paper 2B for BPlan.
  • Paper 1 for BTech/BE: It will have 75 multiple-choice questions (MCQs), carrying a total of 300 marks.
  • Division of Paper 2: Paper 2A for BArch and Paper 2B for BPlan.
    • Paper 2A for BArch: This paper will have 77 questions and carry a total of 400 marks. The drawing section of this paper will be conducted offline.
    • Paper 2B for BPlan: This paper will have 100 MCQs, carrying a total of 400 marks, and will be conducted entirely online.

JEE 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for JEE Main 2025 and JEE Advanced 2025 are outlined below. Candidates must meet specific requirements related to education, age, and attempts to apply for the exams.

JEE Main 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must meet specific requirements to appear for JEE Main 2025:

JEE Main 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria
Criteria Details
Educational Qualification Must have completed Class 12 or an equivalent examination conducted by a recognized board/institution.
Year of Passing Candidates who passed Class 12 in 2023, appearing in 2024, or will appear in 2025 are eligible.
Subjects Required For B.E./B.Tech: Physics, Mathematics, and one of Chemistry/Biology/Technical Vocational Subject.
For B.Arch: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
For B.Plan: Mathematics is mandatory.
Percentage of Marks General category: Minimum 75% marks in Class 12 or top 20 percentile of the respective board.
SC/ST category: Minimum 65% marks.
Age Limit No age limit for JEE Main.
Number of Attempts Maximum of three consecutive years after completing Class 12.

JEE Advanced 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must meet specific requirements to appear for JEE Advanced 2025:

JEE Advanced 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria
Criteria Details
Performance in JEE Main Must be among the top 2,50,000 successful candidates in the B.E./B.Tech (Paper I) of JEE Main 2025.
Category-Wise Distribution GEN-EWS: 10%, OBC-NCL: 27%, SC: 15%, ST: 7.5%, OPEN: 40.5%.
5% horizontal reservation for PwD candidates in all categories.
Age Limit Born on or after October 1, 2000.
SC/ST/PwD: Born on or after October 1, 1995 (5 years relaxation).
Number of Attempts Maximum of two attempts in two consecutive years.
